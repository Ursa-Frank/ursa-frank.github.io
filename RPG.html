Custom RPG

(ongoing)

At the start of 2020, I played my first game of Dungeons and Dragons. My first true Role Playing Game, as the DM no less. Now, I've been playing video games since I was a kid and I've always had an interest in game theory/development/design. So the moment I started playing the game I already had ideas about various elements I wanted to tweak and change. That's where this document comes into place. Somewhere where I can organise my many nitpicks, gripes and complaints and try to work towards some conclusions, be they just a collection of homebrew rules and additions or a full home built RPG. I'm honestly not sure yet

Just to clarify, at the time of writing this, Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition is the only RPG I've played. I am aware of the other editions and roughly what the general opinion on them is, as well as some of the competing RPGs such as Pathfinder. I don't own any of those books yet though, so I don't know if any of the idea's shared in this document overlap with any of those products. That being said, when I do eventually get them I may borrow some idea's



Goals

Well we've got to start somewhere, so how about a list of things I want to improve apon and go from there

In no particular order

  • Expanded List of weapon (including crafted ones)
  • Expand Grappling/wrestling rules
  • Expand the Summoner play style
  • Make Sheilds less boring
  • Halflings and Gnomes are way to similar, Fix that
  • Stop utility Spells breaking curtain Play styles
  • Make more solid rules for mounts and familars
  • Remove Subclasses and just add more classes so multiclassing actually makes sence
  • Balence Multiclassing so its not inherentlly better than solo classing
  • Make Bigger monsters actually feel different
  • Expanded basic attacks
  • Improve Exhaustion
  • Add weapon/armor durabillity
  • Make sure one GM/one PC style games are accounted for in balancing
  • Expand Sidekicks
  • Make PC's feel less "God-like"
  • Make running away strategies more likely to happen and more viable
  • Include more balenced Critical succeeses/fail table (as an optional rule)

Quick Rules

I think the standard rule of using a 20 to check if you succeed in doing something is perfectly fine as is, however I want to include add item durabillity as a feature and I think the best way to introduce that as a mechanic would be as part of the attack roles. So when Rolling to attack, follow the grid to determin extra effects of the attack

RoleDamageTarget
13Attacker's Weapon
21Attacker's Weapon
191Opponent's Armor
203Opponent's Armor
  • The durabillity of weapons and armor is displayed on their respective item tables
  • If the attacker is attacking with a body part, they take the damage from rolling a 1 or 2
  • A Magic user's spellcasting focus is their weapon
  • Natural Armor cannot break
